Access and eResources Library Intern

Job Summary

As a member of the Access and Collection Services department at the Caltech Library, the Access and eResources Library Intern will provide practical, hands-on support in electronic resource management. This individual will work primarily with the Access and eResources Librarian and members of the eResources Task Force (ETF) to maintain the Caltech Library’s access to electronic resources, including e-journals, ebooks, databases, and other digital materials. This position is part of the Elsevier Library Fellowship Internship Program (ELF), and the goal of this program is to support the development of library personnel at the beginning career level in research organizations and advancing the future of the library. As such, the intern will meet virtually with other ELF program participants and with an assigned mentor on a monthly basis, and in-person at a paid-for library conference during the program. This individual will work an average of ten hours per week for the 2023-2024 academic year and is open to a fully remote or hybrid work schedule depending on the individual’s needs and preferences.

Essential Job Duties

  • Perform quality control checks in links and holdings of licensed electronic resources.
  • Troubleshoot, communicate, and resolve technical issues.
  • Organize documents related to electronic resources, such as licenses and KBART files.
  • Assist in gathering usage statistics for annual surveys.
  • Participate in research to further optimize electronic resource access, workflows, and standards.
  • Support outreach to promote eresource awareness among a diverse academic population.
  • Special projects may be created based on the individual’s expertise and interests but will fall within the above duties.

Basic Qualifications

  • Interest in working for libraries and organizations that support academic research.
  • Ability to be proactive and task oriented in the face of changing priorities.
  • Intermediate experience with spreadsheet programs such as Excel and Google Sheets.
  • Must be able to attend the ALA Annual Conference & Exhibition in San Diego, CA, June 27 - July 2, 2024.

Preferred Qualifications

  • Current enrollment or recent graduate in an ALA-accredited master’s program or recent graduate.
  • Experience in and passion for supporting equitable, diverse, and inclusive community spaces.

Required Documents

  • Resume
  • Cover letter that explains career interests, relevant skills, courses, and other experience.

Salary Range

  • This internship pays $22 per hour for ten hours per week for 32 weeks.
  • Additionally, the successful applicant will receive funding to attend an in-person library conference with all ELF participants.

This position is eligible for a remote work option, subject to institutional review and adherence to the Institute’s policies.

  • An approved remote work agreement is required.
  • Additionally, the successful applicant will receive funding to attend an in-person library conference with all ELF participants.
  • In accordance with Caltech’s remote work guidelines, responsible for having a designated work area (home office) and for any costs related to remodeling and/or the initial setup of such designated workspace. Equipment needs for remote work are determined on a case-by-case basis.
  • Experience with Zoom and other similar video/web conference software required.
  • Report to the office as necessary by operational needs.
