Greetings: University Librarian Kara Whatley
Fall is always such a busy time on campus, and the Caltech Library is no exception. You may have seen the spooky movie night we hosted last week in the SFL Amphitheatre or the treats trolley we made available to fuel mid-term study sessions, and those were, as they say, the tip of the iceberg.

We’ve partnered with AAAS to pilot a new type of membership for our researchers, hired Caltech students to work on our NSF-funded upgrades to CaltechDATA , and begun processing the Robert F. Christy papers in the Caltech Archives and Special Collections.
In November, we’re looking forward to the reopened “Becoming Caltech” exhibition , our upcoming slate of Software and Data Carpentries classes, and getting ready for finals. There’s always something going on—in-person and online—at the Caltech Library, and we invite you to join us whenever you can.

Applications for the 2024 ACS LEADS Conference are now open!

Attention high-potential students + early career professionals - ACS is excited to announce that applications for the 2024 ACS LEADS Conference are now open! The LEADS Conference will take place from November 7-9, 2024, in Washington, D.C., and is designed to train people like YOU for successful and impactful careers in the chemical enterprise. LEADS will bring together highly esteemed chemists, professionals, and Nobel Laureates, and will focus on networking, self-reflection, career exploration, mentoring, and technical discussions. If you have a passion for chemistry and are committed to using it to improve your world, we encourage you to learn more about the 2024 ACS LEADS Conference, and apply today!

Cryogenic and Low Temperatures Database (CLTD) Trial

The Library has a two-month trial to the Cryogenic and Low Temperatures Database (CLTD), a relatively new product from CINDAS that launched in 2021. The CLTD offers material characteristics in the cryogenic and low temperature ranges. It consists of thermophysical, mechanical, electrical and other properties of 2,114 materials in 59 materials groups all in the temperature ranges from 0 K to 273 K.

ASME Digital Collection Archive
The Library has bought the electronic journal archive from the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME). The archive dates back to as early as 1930 and includes all published journal content through 1999. Our current ASME subscription includes access to their journals after 2000. This purchase means that we will soon have complete electronic journal holdings from this publisher.

You & Your Thesis

Are you working on your thesis? This November 14 online workshop will give an overview of techniques useful in the production and publication of Caltech electronic theses, including tips on formatting and submitting, intellectual property considerations and access, and thesis dissemination policies. Additional topics may include author identification (ORCIDs) and preservation of thesis-related research data.

Databases & SQL

The Caltech Library is offering an introductory Databases and SQL workshop designed for researchers working with data. This hands-on workshop, spread over two days, will cover selecting, sorting and deduplicating data, creating subsets, calculating derivative data and data aggregations, data formats, and accessing databases from Python programs. The workshop is designed to complement the Managing Data with Pandas workshop.

Managing Data with Pandas

pandas (Python Data Analysis Library) is a fast, powerful, flexible and easy to use data analysis and manipulation tool, built on top of the Python programming language. This one-hour, hands-on online workshop will cover a variety of techniques for selecting, sorting, creating subsets, calculating derivative data, etc. The workshop is designed to complement the Databases and SQL workshop.

Kris Jolley Is Our New Library Facilities Coordinator
Kris Jolley, who has worked at the Library for over twenty years, will be transitioning into a new role as our Library Facilities Coordinator, which means he is joining the Business & Operations team. In this pivotal position, Kris will oversee the comprehensive facilities needs across all our branches. His responsibilities will include helping to maintain the integrity and functionality of our stacks, swiftly addressing any urgent facility-related issues, and the careful planning and management of renovation and repair projects. Congratulations, Kris!

Interview with Archives Intern Gabriela Lazo
Over the summer, thanks to the American Institute of Physics (AIP) Grant for Archives, the Archives hired SJSU MLIS student, Gabriela Lazo, to begin processing the Robert F. Christy Papers. They continued with us through the fall term. Vroman's @ Caltech Library Book Selection: The Warped Side of Our Universe by Kip Thorne & Lia Halloran

Nearly two decades in the making, The Warped Side of Our Universe marks the historic collaboration of Nobel Laureate Kip Thorne and award-winning artist Lia Halloran. It brings to vivid life the wonders and wildness of our universe’s “Warped Side”—objects and phenomena made from warped space and time, from colliding black holes and collapsing wormholes to twisting space vortices and down-cascading time. Read more in the New York Times and see more of Halloran's work when the Archives participates in PST ART 2024 .
