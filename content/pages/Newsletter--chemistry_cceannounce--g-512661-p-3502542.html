Kara Whatley Leaves Caltech Library
At the end of this month, University Librarian Kara Whatley will be leaving to take a new role as Vice Provost of Libraries at the University of Missouri. Since her arrival at Caltech almost five years ago, Kara has made countless contributions to the culture and reputation of the Library. We've posted a farewell from Kara that shares some highlights from her time with us , and we'll greatly miss her guidance and support.

Vice Provost Kaushik Bhattacharya announced Kara's departure to the Caltech community: "Kara has been an important member of our community, and an exceptional leader of our library. She created a sense of mission for our library, spanning traditional roles and emerging technologies, expanded our collection, stabilized our subscriptions, and initiated various collaborations within and outside Caltech. We wish her well in her future endeavors."

Fall 2024 Dissertation Bootcamp Now Open
The Caltech Library and the Graduate Studies Office invite graduate students who are writing their dissertations this Fall to join us for a 2 day writing boot camp on September 19-20, 2024 in the Sherman Fairchild Library on the third floor. The boot camp provides a calm, distraction-free environment for writing, along with refreshments and writing and formatting support. Students are expected to attend both days. Over the two days, optional consultations with the Hixon Writing Center and Caltech Librarians will help students address formatting, copyright, data, and writing questions. Signup sheets will be available starting at breakfast of Day 1. Registration ends Tuesday Sept. 10th at noon PDT!

ACS Virtual Office Hour: "Academia versus Industry"
Thursday Sept. 12th, 9am PDT

During this 90-minute live, virtual session, ACS Career Consultant Lori Spangler will help participants identify their career “sweet spot” through a discussion of the overall market outlook and key responsibilities of the Industry and Academia sectors. After the conclusion of the session, participants will be able to:
* Identify and describe your values and strengths in terms of employability.
* Compare and contrast job market outlook and skillsets in industry and academia.
* Discover which employment sector best matches your values and strengths.
The presentation will be followed by the opportunity for attendees to network in small groups led by ACS Career Consultants and received personalized career advice.

Kristin Briney Wins RDAP Work of the Year

Congratulations to Biology and Biological Engineering Library Kristin Briney, whose Caltech Library publication The Research Data Management Workbook has been awarded the first “Work of the Year” recognition by RDAP , the Research Data Access & Preservation Association! These awards celebrate significant contributions to the Association and to the field of research data access and preservation.

In the announcement of the RDAP awards , they write of The Research Data Management Workbook, a collection of exercises designed to help researchers improve their data management: "This work is an excellent example of the significant work that our RDAP members contribute to, and we offer a huge congratulations to Kristin!"

Recent Publication by University Archivist Peter Collopy

An essay by University Archivist Peter Collopy is included in the recently published anthology Modernism, Art, Therapy from Yale University Press, edited by Suzanne Hudson and Tanya Sheehan. His contribution, "Between Paradigms: Video and Art Therapy," "traces how [video therapy] developed at New York’s Village Project and elsewhere in the United States from about 1967 to 1975. It considers how painter Frank Gillette and philosopher-sociologist Gioscia in particular drew from both modernist and emerging postmodernist intellectual currents, particularly the boundary-crossing work of media theorist Marshall McLuhan, to navigate the intersections of disciplines."


Dark Angel Jack Parsons
Dive into the mystique of Marjorie Cameron's "Dark Angel," a striking portrayal of her late husband, Jack Parsons. Painted against a neutral backdrop, Parsons appears as a celestial figure, part winged Lucifer, part risen Christ, enveloped in a billowing black robe with an electrifying gaze. Did you know? Parsons was a founding member of the GALCIT Rocket Research Group, the blueprint for NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL).
We'll be sharing sneak peeks into "Crossing Over: Art and Science at Caltech, 1920-2020" at the Archives' Instagram account . Coming to campus on September 27, 2024!

GeoRef

The Library has acquired GeoRef using Elsevier’s Engineering Village platform. GeoRef is the most in-depth abstracting and indexing (A&I) database available for geoscience research. GeoRef contains over 4.6 million records, from 1666 to the present for North America, as well as global coverage dating back to 1933: references to U.S. Geological Survey publications, theses and dissertations, books, maps, reports, as well as 3,500+ journals. To access GeoRef Engineering Village's, please use these links: off-campus access (log-in with Access credentials) or on-campus (or on VPN).
