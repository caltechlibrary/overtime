What's New: #PSTSneakPeak

This month, we've started sharing a sneak peak into our Crossing Over: Art and Science at Caltech, 1920-2020 exhibition for PST2024 at the Caltech Archives Instagram account.

In this #PSTsneakpeak above, you'll see a massive drawing of the two-hundred-inch telescope at Mt. Palomar. This highlight of the Caltech Archives' collection is an iconic example of the "cutaway" work of Russell W. Porter. Artist, architect, and armature telescope maker, Porter was known for his ability to precisely portray mechanical objects, making him a valuable asset to the Palomar project team. You can see this and other examples of Russell Porter's work in our upcoming exhibition Crossing Over: Art and Science at Caltech, 1920-2020 opening this fall.

Funder Compliance Support

Grant recipients are regularly asked by their funding agencies to provide reports (progress or final) that collect and present information following meticulous guidelines. To assist with these pursuits, library staff are available to work with principal investigators, grant managers, and division administrators to ensure they meet compliance requirements to receive and maintain funding from these government agencies. The Library can assist with collaborator reports, data management plans, manuscript and data deposit, ORCID profiles, and other required documentation.

The Company of Biologists
Read & Publish Agreement

As of 2024, the Library is adding an additional two The Company of Biologists titles to our Read & Publish Agreement. Since 2021, Caltech authors have enjoyed free, Open Access publishing in three The Company of Biologist titles. Starting in January, Caltech corresponding authors publishing in Disease Models & Mechanisms and Biology Open will be able to enjoy Open Access publishing without any author processing charges (APCs).

Data Visualization 101, and Interactive Data Visualizations in Python

Want to improve your data visualizations? The first day (February 21) of this online workshop will cover how to choose the right chart type for your data and how good design choices will make your chart easier to understand. The focus will be on visualization best practices, independent of any specific visualization software. The rest of the workshop (February 22-23) is an introduction to making interactive data visualizations in Python. Register here .

Inventions, Patents, and Licensing (March 12, 2024)

The Office of Technology Transfer and Corporate Partnerships presents an overview of different kinds of intellectual property (IP) and how they are managed at Caltech, with an emphasis on patents as a means of protecting inventions developed in the course of Caltech research. Ownership and licensing of Caltech IP is also addressed as well as a brief overview of patent searching, locating English language patent equivalents, legal status issues, and current awareness techniques. Register here.

You & Your Thesis
(March 12, 2024)

Are you working on your thesis? This February 21 online workshop will give an overview of techniques useful in the production and publication of Caltech electronic theses, including tips on formatting and submitting, intellectual property considerations and access, and thesis dissemination policies. Additional topics may include author identification (ORCIDs) and preservation of thesis-related research data. Register here.

2023 DATAWorks! Prize

The Caltech Library was awarded a 2023 DATAWorks! Prize for Biology & Biological Engineering Librarian Kristin Briney's file naming convention worksheet . Kristin said, "I’m excited to share this tool with everyone as I firmly believe that naming data files descriptively and consistently makes it so much easier to reuse that data in the future."

Matt Buga Moves to CCE
After over seven years of working for the Caltech Library on the Business & Operations team, Matt Buga will be moving over to a new administrative position for the division of Chemistry & Chemical Engineering (CCE). Matt will provide administrative and clerical support to several research groups within CCE. He will continue to work with purchasing, coordinating events, maintaining calendars, and other odds and ends. He is excited to take on new business operations duties related to patents, reports, grant proposals, classroom support, postdoc & research staff hiring, and more. We will miss him, but we're happy to still see him around the Institute. Congratulations, Matt!

The Curious Mr. Feynman
A few months ago, Freakonomics Radio reached out to the Caltech Archives to get access to the Richard Feynman Papers. Audiovisual Collections Archivist Elisa Piccio and Digital Archivist Richard Thai helped them, while the host interviewed Michelle Feynman. Caltech Archives gets a mention and Elisa and Richard are thanked by name! The podcast is called The Curious Mr. Feynman and we appear in part one of the three-part series. "From the Manhattan Project to the Challenger investigation, the physicist Richard Feynman loved to shoot down what he called 'lousy ideas.' Today, the world is awash in lousy ideas — so maybe it’s time to get some more Feynman in our lives?"

Towards the Black Hole
In August, the Archives loaned a notebook of technical notes from the H.P. Robertson papers (1936-1941) to Rijksmuseum Boerhaave for an exhibition on black holes . "Dichter bij het Zwarte Gat" is a guided tour through this fascinating subject. Stop by if you happen to be in Leiden before January 5, 2025.
