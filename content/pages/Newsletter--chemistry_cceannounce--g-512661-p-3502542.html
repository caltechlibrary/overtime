Caltech Archives Joins PST ART 2024

Caltech Archives is excited to be part of PST ART : Art & Science Collide , a Getty initiative creating "opportunities for civic dialogue around some of the most urgent problems of our time by exploring past and present connections between art and science in a series of exhibitions, public programs, and other resources." How have art, architecture, and scientific images been part of discovery and invention at Caltech over the last century? Mark your calendars for our exhibition Crossing Over: Art and Science at Caltech , 1920–2020 , which will open to the public in four spaces on our campus in September 2024.

CCDC Webinar: Boosting Research Efficiency in Industry with FAIR Data Thursday May 25, 2023 - 8:00 AM - 9:00 AM PDT

In this webinar, we’ll explore how making proprietary data more FAIR (findable, accessible, interoperable, reusable) has benefitted industrial research teams and enabled public data. By working towards FAIR data principles, existing and historical findings can be used to inform and guide new work. S uch data-driven insights strengthen confidence during drug discovery, development, and materials design. You will learn: How does the chemical industry benefit from the FAIR data principles that underpin all CCDC offerings? How can proprietary data be made more FAIR to help advance R&D goals?
Registration: https://register.gotowebinar.com/register/7792517509853356559
Webinar: What is digital IUPAC? Tools for an increasingly digital research culture Wednesday, May 31, 2023 - 09 :00 AM - 10:00 AM PDT

Fast forward not so far into the future where electronic laboratory notebooks are used widely and research data are published with your work, where artificial intelligence and machine learning applications like ChatGPT can suck up that data into new discoveries. Join us for a conversation among experts in the chemical data management field with younger chemists about: Managing and sharing research data in the cloud (or "as digital data") Standards, identifiers, and metadata in a FAIR world emerging resource to help manage/clean/prep/check your data.
For registration: https://register.gotowebinar.com/register/7988794630567972704

Featured Resource: Audio Abstracts
As of April 2023, bioRxiv and PaperPlayer now offer preprint audio abstracts. Readers can  listen to audio versions of neuroscience abstracts, increasing accessibility to the content and providing alternative modes for consuming the abstracts of scientific manuscripts. Clicking on the Audio abstract link will open an audio player control, which allows automatic play through ten abstracts in the Neuroscience collection at a time.

The Library has a new transformative agreement with Cold Spring Harbor Press. The agreement means that the Library’s subscription covers both reading and publishing open access in specific CSH titles. Featured Resource: DEI in GPS
The GPS Division’s DEI team has curated a bibliography of articles related to DEI within the geological sciences. Sample titles include “Scientists from Historically Excluded Groups Face a Hostile Obstacle Course,” “Race and Racism in the Geosciences,” and “Ten Simple Rules for Building an Antiracist Lab.” A collection of some printed articles is now on display in the Geology Library reading room. Some of the students involved with this reference library include Koehne Tobias, Lilian Dove, Clare Singer, and MJ Wilner.

Vroman's @ Caltech Library Book Selection: Molecular Astrophysics by A. G. G. M. Tielens

Focusing on the organic inventory of regions of star and planet formation in the interstellar medium of galaxies, this comprehensive overview of the molecular universe is an invaluable reference source for advanced undergraduates through to entry-level researchers. The physical and chemical properties of gaseous molecules, mixed molecular ices, and large polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon molecules and fullerenes and their role in the interstellar medium are highlighted. For those with an interest in the molecular universe, this advanced textbook bridges the gap between molecular physics, astronomy, and physical chemistry.
