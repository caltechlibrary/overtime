Caltech Library Government Documents

Caltech does not have a separate and discrete publicly-accessible U.S. government documents collection. Caltech is selective and integrates the material into the overall collection and in recent years puts priority on electronic resources.

For assistance with obtaining government information:

  • Use this guide
  • Submit your question . We will respond as soon as we can.
  • Text us: 626-587-4833
  • Search for an answer to your question in our FAQ.

Databases and More

Millions of US Government documents are born digital or being digitized, and links to agencies may lead you to the sources you need. NOTE: Please see the preceding tab for the Basic Core Collection Government Resources for more searching resources.

  • A-Z List of Executive Agencies
    List of U.S. government executive agencies and departments
  • Law Library Microform Consortium - LLMC Digital
    Preserves legal titles and government documents including many federal, state, and international government and legal documents. Includes indigenous law as well.
    Subjects include:

    U.S. Federal Government
    U.S. States and Territories
    Canada
    Germany - Federal/Natl Government
    Germany - States, Kingdoms, Free Cities
    United Kingdom
    Other Countries
    Indigenous Law
    International Law and Organizations
    Special Focus Collections
  • LexisNexis Congressional
    Publications of the U.S. Congress. Now handled by Nexis Uni database. (access required via Caltech Access credentials)
  • Library of Congress
    Explore the resources and collections of the Library of Congress.
  • LocatorPlus
    Search the collections of the National Library of Medicine.
  • Medical Subject Headings (MeSH)
    Search the MeSH headings which are used to provide a hierarchically-organized terminology for indexing and cataloging biomedical information.
  • MetaLib
    MetaLib is a federated search engine that searches multiple U.S. Federal government databases, retrieving reports, articles, and citations while providing direct links to selected resources available online.
  • NASA Technical Reports Server
    Provides access to aerospace citations, full-text documents, images, and videos.
  • National Archives Records
    Research the records of the National Archives and Records Administration.
  • Science.gov
    Searches over 60 databases and over 2200 selected websites from 15 federal agencies, offering 200 million pages of authoritative U.S. government science information including research and development results.
  • TRAIL (Technical Report Archive & Image Library)
    Provides unrestricted access to U.S. Government agency technical reports.
