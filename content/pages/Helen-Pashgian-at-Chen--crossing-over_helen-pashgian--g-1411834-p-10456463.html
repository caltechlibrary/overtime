Crossing Over: Art and Science at Caltech, 1920–⁠2020: Helen Pashgian at Chen

The exhibition concludes with a special installation by acclaimed Light and Space artist Helen Pashgian. One of only two female members of the Southern California art movement (with Mary Morse), Pashgian was the lone woman to join Caltech’s newly created artist-in-residence program in 1969. She exhibited her first resin-based lenses at Caltech’s Baxter Art Gallery two years later, together with translucent works by sculptors Robert Bassler, Peter Alexander, and David Elder. Pashgian’s radiant Lens of 2023, displayed in a temporary pavilion at Caltech’s Chen Institute for Neuroscience, reflects her longstanding interest in the effects and perception of light while attesting to the significance of her path-breaking experiments with polyester resins at Caltech over fifty years ago. “One must move around to observe changes,” the artists notes about the shifting appearance of her transformative works. “Coming and going, appearing and receding, visible and invisible—a phenomenon of constant movement. It touches on the mysterious, the place beyond which the eye cannot go.” (Image via New York Times )
