At Caltech, we acknowledge that the Institute sits in the traditional territory and homeland of the Gabrielino Tongva people. Indigenous Peoples' Day is a day to celebrate and honor the Gabrielino Tongva and all the Indigenous people whose land we occupy and share.

In addition to the materials we have on display to borrow from the Caltech Library, the lending library at the Caltech Center for Inclusion and Diversity has additional materials to expand your knowledge of Indigenous histories and cultures.

Find out more about the local events in the Los Angeles area open to the public.

Oyate Woyaka (The People Speak) Documentary Screening and Reception at the Autry Museum of the American West

The Autry Museum in Griffith Park | 4700 Western Heritage Way, Los Angeles, CA 90027-1462

Oyate Woyaka features fluent speakers of the Lakota language. Because of government policies to intentionally destroy the language, Lakota is nearing extinction. This film shows this history as well as the efforts being made to preserve and revitalize the ancestral language.

Indigenous Peoples’ Day 2024

Getty Center | 1200 Getty Center Drive, Los Angeles, CA 90049

In honor of Indigenous Peoples’ Day, Getty hosts its third annual family event to learn about Southern California's vibrant Native American cultures. The theme this year celebrates flora, with activities sharing traditional and contemporary Indigenous perspectives and narratives.

The CCID and Caltech Library will have book displays up for the week of October 10-18, 2024.
