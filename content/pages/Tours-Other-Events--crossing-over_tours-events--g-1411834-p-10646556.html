December 4: Open Tour

Join exhibition organizers for a tour of Crossing Over: Art and Science at Caltech, 1920–2020 . How, this exhibition asks, have Caltech scientists and engineers used images and collaborated with artists to discover, invent, and communicate? The result, unfolding across Caltech’s campus in six outdoor and indoor venues, weaves together the history of science with historical and contemporary art. Sections focus on astronomy, seismology, genetics, chemistry, particle physics, nuclear weapons, data visualization, rocketry, and planetary science. The tour starts at 2:30 pm on Bechtel Mall , just east of S. Wilson and west of Caltech Hall at the tall white Crossing Over sign. Register for the tour.

December 6: Caltech History & Architectural Tour

Docents will meet attendees opposite 525 S. Wilson Avenue, on the northeast corner of S. Wilson Avenue and E. California Blvd, for a free 90 minute architectural tour of Caltech's beautiful campus along with viewing the Crossing Over: Art and Science at Caltech, 1920–2020 exhibit. Advance RSVP required . Tour attendance is limited. Please arrive early to allow time for finding parking at Caltech. Tour will leave promptly at 11:00 a.m.

December 14: Caltech PST ART Symposia Day

Join us for in-depth exploration and conversation at the intersection of art and science in the final weekend of the Getty-initiated PST ART: Art & Science Collide regional event at Caltech. Artists and researchers from the Caltech Archives, JPL, and CaltechLive will each host 90-minute presentations that illuminate and celebrate the many ways in which scientists and artists share in the creative process and innovation, and how their work contributes to the welfare of our communities.

The presentations are free and open to the public in Ramo Auditorium. All events are presented with the support of the James Michelin Distinguished Lecture Program in the Division of the Humanities and Social Sciences . Registration for each presentation is strongly recommended.
